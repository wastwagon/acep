Ghana's Petroleum Revenue Management Framework

Overview of the institutional framework and governance mechanisms established to ensure effective management of Ghana's petroleum resources.

The Importance of Effective Revenue Management

Ghana's petroleum revenue management strategy is pivotal to transforming finite natural resources into sustainable economic development. Effective management of these revenues safeguards intergenerational equity, fosters economic resilience, and protects against resource curse phenomena such as corruption, patronage, and economic volatility. Since the enactment of the Petroleum Revenue Management Act (PRMA) in 2011, Ghana has established a governance framework that prioritizes transparency, accountability, and public participation to ensure that petroleum revenues contribute meaningfully to national development while supporting future generations. This approach has positioned Ghana as a regional model for responsible resource governance, with the PRMA's provisions for strict allocation formulas, public reporting, and independent oversight helping to mitigate the risks typically associated with natural resource wealth.

Key Institutions
  • Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA) - Collects taxes, royalties, and other fiscal payments from petroleum operations
  • Ghana National Petroleum Corporation (GNPC) - Receives production entitlements on behalf of the state
Revenue Sources
  • Royalties - Fixed percentage of gross production (typically 5% for oil, 3-5% for gas)
  • Carried & Participating Interest - State's share in petroleum operations (minimum 15%)
  • Corporate Income Tax - 35% on petroleum operations
  • Additional Oil Entitlements (AOE) - Progressive resource rent tax based on profitability
  • Surface Rentals - Charges for the area covered by petroleum agreements
Legal Framework

Revenue collection is governed by:

  • Petroleum Revenue Management Act (PRMA), 2011 (Act 815)
  • Petroleum (Exploration and Production) Act, 2016 (Act 919)
  • Income Tax Act, 2015 (Act 896)

Petroleum Holding Fund
×
Purpose & Management

The Petroleum Holding Fund (PHF) is a transitional fund established under Section 2 of the PRMA as the initial repository for all petroleum receipts. It serves as a clearinghouse before revenue is distributed according to the allocation formula.

Managing Institution

The Bank of Ghana is responsible for operating the PHF, including:

  • Receiving all petroleum revenues from the GRA and other sources
  • Maintaining proper books of accounts and records
  • Presenting reports on the PHF to the Minister of Finance
  • Transferring funds according to the disbursement formula established by the PRMA
Key Provisions
  • No encumbrance or borrowing against the PHF is permitted
  • No direct service of loans from the PHF
  • Transfers out of the PHF must follow the allocation formula prescribed by the PRMA
  • Maximum 7 working days holding period before transfers must be completed

Ghana National Petroleum Corporation
×
Role & Mandate

GNPC is Ghana's national oil company established by PNDC Law 64 of 1983. It receives 15% of petroleum revenues to fund its operations and investments.

Activities Funded
  • Equity Financing - Funding Ghana's participating interest in petroleum operations
  • Exploration Activities - Investment in new exploration to discover additional petroleum reserves
  • Technical Operations - Support for existing field development and production activities
  • Capacity Building - Training and development of Ghanaian expertise in petroleum operations
Governance
  • GNPC must submit annual program of activities to Parliament for approval
  • Funding is capped at 30% of state's carried and participating interest
  • Required to publish annual reports on fund utilization
  • Subject to audit by the Auditor-General

Structure & Purpose

The Ghana Petroleum Funds (GPF) comprise 15% of petroleum revenues and are divided into two separate funds with distinct purposes:

Ghana Stabilisation Fund (GSF)
70% of GPF
  • Cushions budget impact when petroleum revenues fall short of forecast
  • Provides short to medium-term economic stability
  • Managed for liquidity and moderate returns
  • Current balance: $826.4 million
Ghana Heritage Fund (GHF)
30% of GPF
  • Endowment fund for future generations
  • Cannot be withdrawn until petroleum reserves are depleted
  • Managed for long-term capital growth
  • Current balance: $293.3 million
Management & Investment
  • Bank of Ghana manages both funds according to investment guidelines
  • Investment Advisory Committee provides guidance on investment strategy
  • Funds are invested in qualifying instruments approved by the Minister of Finance
  • Annual reports on fund performance are published

Ghana Stabilisation Fund
×
Purpose

The Ghana Stabilisation Fund (GSF) is designed to cushion the impact on or sustain public expenditure capacity during periods of unanticipated petroleum revenue shortfalls.

Key Features
  • Receives 70% of the Ghana Petroleum Funds allocation (10.5% of total petroleum revenue)
  • Managed for higher liquidity to enable quick access when needed
  • Can be drawn upon when petroleum revenues fall below 25% of the forecast
  • Maximum withdrawal in a financial year is 75% of the annual accrued amount
  • Has a cap that when reached, excess funds are used to retire debt or allocated to contingency
Investment Strategy

The Stabilisation Fund is invested in:

  • Short to medium-term highly liquid investments
  • Low-risk securities and financial instruments
  • Foreign currency assets to provide currency diversification

Ghana Heritage Fund
×
Purpose

The Ghana Heritage Fund (GHF) serves as an endowment fund for future generations that will support development when petroleum reserves are depleted.

Key Features
  • Receives 30% of the Ghana Petroleum Funds allocation (4.5% of total petroleum revenue)
  • No withdrawals permitted until petroleum reserves are depleted
  • Principal and interest accumulated are preserved until petroleum reserves are exhausted
  • Only Parliament can authorize use, and only after certification that petroleum reserves are depleted
  • Annual returns are reinvested to grow the fund value
Investment Strategy

The Heritage Fund is invested in:

  • Long-term higher-yielding investments
  • Diversified portfolio including equities and other growth assets
  • International financial instruments that provide geographic diversification
  • Assets that offer protection against inflation

Oversight Framework

Ghana's petroleum revenue management is subject to multiple layers of oversight to ensure transparency, accountability, and compliance with the PRMA.

Public Interest and Accountability Committee (PIAC)
Mandate:
  • Monitor and evaluate compliance with the PRMA
  • Provide independent assessment of petroleum revenue management
  • Publish semi-annual and annual reports
  • Engage with citizens and stakeholders
Composition:

13 members representing various constituencies including civil society, professional bodies, traditional authorities, and religious groups

Parliament
Mandate:
  • Approve annual ABFA priority areas
  • Approve GNPC's program of activities
  • Review reports from Ministry of Finance, PIAC, and Auditor-General
  • Hold public hearings on petroleum revenue management
  • Approve withdrawals from the Ghana Petroleum Funds
Key Committee:

Finance Committee and Public Accounts Committee

Auditor-General
Mandate:
  • Conduct annual audits of the Petroleum Holding Fund
  • Audit the Ghana Petroleum Funds
  • Verify ABFA utilization
  • Audit GNPC's use of allocated funds
  • Submit audit reports to Parliament
Audit Scope:

Financial, compliance, and performance audits

Civil Society & Media
Role:
  • Independent monitoring of petroleum revenue management
  • Advocacy for transparency and compliance with PRMA
  • Public education and awareness raising
  • Research and policy recommendations
Key Organizations:

Ghana Extractive Industries Transparency Initiative (GHEITI), Natural Resource Governance Institute (NRGI), Africa Centre for Energy Policy (ACEP)
